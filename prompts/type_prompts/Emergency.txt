<session_module type="Emergency" version="3.0">
  <purpose>
    An acute/crisis presentation. Centre of gravity: the precipitant, current risk, and immediate management.
    Speed and risk clarity matter more than breadth — but every line still needs transcript support.
  </purpose>

  <clinical_trajectory>
    Open with the central acute problem and the precipitant driving this presentation.
  </clinical_trajectory>

  <emphasis>
    If the transcript refers to an emergency or risk event that occurred before this contact, surface it explicitly
    (core source_fidelity). Risk is the priority field-set — do not omit Risk Assessment when any risk content exists.
  </emphasis>

  <section name="Chief Complaints / HOPI / Stressors">
    Primary symptoms, duration, mode of onset (§onset), course, impact on functioning, precipitating factors — as stated.
    The precipitating event lives here once (core one_fact_one_place); reference it elsewhere rather than repeating.
  </section>

  <section name="Risk Assessment">
    The core of an emergency note. Ideation / Plan / Intent / Means per §risk.ideation; quote any ideation, intent, or means
    statement VERBATIM (core quotation_discipline). Past risk behaviours, triggers, protective factors as stated.
    Risk Level and Action Taken are clinician-assigned only; never invent a level. Add a Watch / Escalate forward line.
  </section>

  <section name="MSE (Only Positive Findings)">
    Positive findings only; audio-only so no visual cues. Speech = rate/tone/volume; thought patterns → Thought Process,
    their content → Thought Content. Insight/Judgment only if assessed.
  </section>

  <section name="Provisional Diagnosis / Intervention Done / Immediate Management Plan">
    Diagnosis only if the clinician stated one. Interventions and safety measures as brief items.
    Parental/guardian communication and referrals as owner–action–trigger (core actions).
  </section>

  <controlled_vocabularies>
    Reference only — never output. Use verbatim or omit.
    §onset: Sudden, Gradual
    §risk.type: Passive suicidal ideation, Active ideation with plan, Self-harm/NSSI, Harm to others, Medical risk, Neglect, None identified
    §risk.ideation: Ideation (Present/Absent/Passive/Active) · Plan (Present/Absent/Vague/Specific) · Intent (Present/Absent/Ambivalent) · Means (Yes/No/Unknown) · Hopelessness (Present/Absent/Not assessed)
    §risk.action: Monitoring/Safety plan, Safety contract, Psychiatric referral, Emergency escalation, Family informed (with consent), Means restriction advised, Hospitalisation arranged
    Abuse disclosures documented verbatim, never softened.
  </controlled_vocabularies>
</session_module>
