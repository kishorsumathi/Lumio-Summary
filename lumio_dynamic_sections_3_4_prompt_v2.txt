<section id="4" name="STANDARD-SCREENS CHECKLISTS">

These are the fixed lists of screening items an experienced clinician would routinely elicit for each major diagnostic category. Section number and item tags align: every checklist below is tagged §4.1.1.x. The lists are owned by Clinical (Dr V) and versioned independently; use them verbatim.
Use the checklist for the working diagnostic context as a coverage map: for every checklist item the transcript addresses, ensure the corresponding finding is captured in the note. For every checklist item the transcript does not address, flag it briefly as a gap if it is clinically live — do not pad with “not asked” entries for routine items.
When the clinician’s stated diagnosis or working impression maps to more than one category, use the de-duplicated union of the relevant checklists. When no diagnosis or impression is stated, use the Universal screens (§4.1.1.7) only.
§4.1.1.1 Depression-spectrum presentations
Sleep · Appetite · Energy · Anhedonia (loss of interest in usually-pleasurable activities) · Concentration · Guilt / worthlessness · Psychomotor changes · Suicidal ideation · Past depressive episodes
§4.1.1.2 Anxiety-spectrum presentations
Worry content and triggers · Physical symptoms (palpitations, sweating, dizziness) · Avoidance behaviours · Panic attacks (frequency, duration, triggers) · Sleep disturbance · Substance use as coping · Functional impact (work, study, social) · Family history of anxiety
§4.1.1.3 OCD presentations
Specific obsessions and their content · Specific compulsions and their content · Time spent daily on obsessions/compulsions · Functional impact · Insight into senselessness of compulsions · Past trial of SSRI and response · Family history of OCD
§4.1.1.4 Psychosis-spectrum presentations
Auditory hallucinations (presence, content, command quality) · Visual hallucinations · Other-modality hallucinations · Delusions (sub-type, fixity) · Passivity phenomena · Negative symptoms (alogia, anhedonia, avolition) · Premorbid functioning · Family history of psychosis · Substance use
§4.1.1.5 Trauma / PTSD presentations
Index trauma description · Intrusive symptoms · Avoidance · Negative cognitions and mood · Hyperarousal · Sleep / nightmares · Dissociative symptoms · Functional impact · Abuse history (physical, sexual, emotional, witnessed — document as disclosed, preserve patient's words verbatim)
§4.1.1.6 Substance-use presentations
Substance categories used · Frequency, quantity, duration · Withdrawal symptoms · Tolerance · Failed attempts to reduce · Functional impact · Co-use of multiple substances · Family history
§4.1.1.7 Universal screens (apply to all presentations)
Suicidal ideation (passive and active) · Self-harm history · Abuse history (current and historical — ask explicitly; do not assume absence) · Past psychiatric history · Past treatment and response · Current medications · Substance use (at least a quick screen) · Family psychiatric history · Functional baseline
